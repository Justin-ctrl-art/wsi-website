Funds one facilitated peer group session where survivors find language for what they carry — and recognize they are not alone.

Other Ways to Give

Thoughtful giving, on your terms.

Monthly giving

Sustain the long arc of recovery. Monthly gifts — however modest — give us the steady footing to plan clinical care years in advance.

Stock & securities

A gift of appreciated stock can extend your generosity while offering meaningful tax benefits. Our development team handles every transfer with care.

Planned giving & legacy gifts

Include the Institute in your will or estate plan. A planned gift is a quiet promise that healing outlasts a single lifetime.

Corporate matching

Many employers will match your gift — sometimes doubling or tripling it. Ask your HR team, or contact us and we'll help you confirm eligibility.

Volunteer

Bring what you have. We'll find a place for it.

Our volunteers are the quiet infrastructure of this work — clinicians, writers, translators, and neighbors who show up with skill and steadiness. All volunteers complete a brief trauma-informed orientation before beginning.

Apply to Volunteer
  • 01

    Clinical volunteer

    Licensed clinicians offering supervised hours in peer-support, group therapy, or our telehealth program.

  • 02

    Storytelling & communications

    Writers, editors, and designers who help us share survivor stories with the dignity and care they deserve.

  • 03

    Event support

    Help host community gatherings, fundraising events, and clinician convenings in a warm, trauma-aware environment.

  • 04

    Translation & interpretation

    Bilingual speakers — especially in Arabic, Dari, Pashto, Ukrainian, Tigrinya, and Spanish — who can bridge language in clinical and community settings.

  • 05

    Administrative & operations

    Behind-the-scenes roles in scheduling, data entry, grant research, and donor stewardship — quiet work that keeps the institute running.

Corporate Sponsorship

Partnerships of consequence.

We work with a small group of corporate and institutional partners whose values align with ours. Sponsorship is thoughtful, long-term, and built around measurable impact.

Contact Our Partnerships Team

  • Named program support

    Underwrite a specific clinical program, fellowship cohort, or community initiative — with clear, annual reporting on outcomes.

  • Employee engagement

    Trauma-aware workshops, volunteer days, and matched giving programs that give your teams a tangible way to participate.

  • Shared recognition

    Acknowledgment in our annual report, co-branded content where appropriate, and invitations to our convenings — always on terms that honor survivor privacy first.

  • Research & policy collaboration

    For mission-aligned partners, opportunities to co-fund research, policy briefs, and public scholarship that widen the field.

Share Your Story

If you are ready, we will listen.

Your story belongs to you. If you choose to share part of it with us, we will receive it with care — and only publish what you approve, in the form you approve.

Every story we share goes through a consent-first editorial process. Names can be changed, details withheld, and context adjusted to protect you and those you love. You may withdraw consent at any time, for any reason.

  • —Full consent, reviewed at every stage
  • —Privacy and identity protection by default
  • —Editorial care from trauma-informed writers
  • —The right to withdraw, always
