Programs & Services

Care that meets survivors where they are.

Clinical services, peer community, and clinician education — held together by one premise: recovery from war is a long craft, and survivors deserve a place patient enough to do it well.

Pillar 01 · Healing

Clinical care for the injuries war leaves behind.

Our clinical program is the Institute's core. A multidisciplinary team of psychiatrists, psychologists, social workers, and integrative practitioners works with survivors over months — sometimes years — using the therapies that the best available evidence, and the person in front of us, say will help.

We treat PTSD, complex trauma, moral injury, grief, and the traumatic brain injuries that often travel alongside them.

  • ·Trauma-focused psychotherapy — including CPT, PE, EMDR, and narrative exposure tailored to each survivor's history and pace.
  • ·Psychiatric care — medication evaluation and management in partnership with primary care providers.
  • ·Integrative healing — somatic therapy, acupuncture, and culturally grounded practices offered alongside conventional care.
  • ·Family and couples support — because trauma is rarely held by one person alone.

Pillar 02 · Connecting

Community as medicine.

Trauma isolates. One of the most reliable findings of the last forty years of research on recovery is that survivors heal faster, and more durably, in the company of others who understand. Our Connecting programs build those rooms — and then step quietly out of the way.

We convene veterans, refugees, and civilians in settings that are structured enough to be safe and open enough to be real.

  • ·Peer support circles — small, facilitated groups for veterans and civilians, meeting weekly or biweekly.
  • ·Refugee family programs — multigenerational gatherings that combine clinical check-ins with shared meals and practical support.
  • ·Cross-cultural dialogue — structured encounters between veterans and civilians from conflicts the U.S. has been party to.
  • ·Memorial and ritual work — quiet, survivor-designed gatherings that mark anniversaries and losses.

Pillar 03 · Educating

Teaching the next room full of clinicians.

Most survivors of war never reach a specialty clinic. They see a primary care doctor, a school counselor, a resettlement caseworker. Our Educating pillar exists to make sure that, wherever a survivor lands, someone in the room has been trained to recognize what they are carrying.

We publish, we teach, and we put our screening tools in the hands of the people who need them.

  • ·Clinician fellowship — a year-long, mentored training program in trauma-informed care.
  • ·RHS-15 screening tool — free, validated in more than twelve languages, now used in primary care and resettlement worldwide.
  • ·Continuing education — accredited workshops for physicians, psychologists, social workers, and nurses.
  • ·Peer-reviewed research — publications in refugee mental health, screening science, and complex trauma.

Featured Projects

Work in the world.

Long-running partnerships — some local, some global — that translate our three pillars into programs that travel.

International · Since 2014

RHS-15 Sweden Project

A partnership with the Karolinska Institutet and Swedish county councils to implement the Refugee Health Screener-15 in primary care across seven regions. The project has since shaped national guidance on refugee mental health screening.

In partnership with Karolinska Institutet & Region Stockholm

International · Since 2017

Uganda Program

Community-based healing circles and clinician education in partnership with Ugandan mental health providers in the West Nile region, serving South Sudanese refugees and Ugandan nationals alongside one another.

In partnership with the Peter C. Alderman Foundation

Domestic · Since 2011

Veterans Reintegration Circles

Small, facilitated peer groups for post-9/11 combat veterans, meeting weekly across six U.S. cities. Built in consultation with veterans themselves, and independently evaluated for durable reductions in isolation and substance use.

In partnership with regional VA Medical Centers

Education · Annual

Clinician Training Institute

A residential week of advanced training in trauma-informed care for physicians, psychologists, and social workers. Two hundred clinicians have completed the program; many now run their own war-survivor services.

Accredited for 42 CME/CE hours

Signature Tool

The Refugee Health
Screener-15.

15 questions. Neutral language. Widely adopted across U.S. and international refugee health.

Dr. Hollifield led development of the RHS-15 with the Pathways to Wellness partners — Beth Farmer, Junko Yamazaki, Tsegaba Woldehaimanot, Sasha Verbillis-Kolp, and Annette Holland.

The 15-item mental health screener was designed for ease of use in healthcare settings: short, with neutral language that does not directly reference violence, torture, or trauma. It was empirically developed as a valid, efficient, and effective screener for common mental disorders in refugees.

Today the RHS-15 is integrated into standard physical health screenings for newly arrived refugees in many U.S. states, and used internationally with asylum seekers in Sweden and across Europe.
