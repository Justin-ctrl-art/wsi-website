FAQ

Questions we hear often.

If your question isn't here, write to us directly. A real person will read your note.

How can I refer someone to your programs?
Referrals can come from the survivor themselves, a family member, or a clinician. Begin by emailing programs@warsurvivors.org with a brief note — no clinical details needed at first. Our intake team will follow up within two business days to walk you gently through the next steps.
Do you offer services internationally?
Yes. We partner with clinicians and community organizations in fifteen countries, with a particular focus on North America, Europe, the Middle East, and East Africa. Some services are delivered directly via telehealth; others are offered through trained partner organizations embedded in local communities.
Is my donation tax-deductible?
Yes. The War Survivors Institute is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization in the United States. All gifts are tax-deductible to the fullest extent of the law. For donors outside the US, we can coordinate with international giving partners — please email development@warsurvivors.org.
How do I volunteer?
Visit our Get Involved page to see current opportunities across clinical, storytelling, events, translation, and administrative roles. All volunteers complete a brief trauma-informed orientation before beginning — this protects the community we serve, and helps you arrive with confidence.
Do you offer clinician training?
Yes. Our clinician fellowship is a structured program in trauma-informed, evidence-based care for professionals working with veterans, civilians, and refugees. We accept a small cohort each year. Continuing-education workshops and consultation hours are available for practicing clinicians throughout the year.
Can I visit the Institute in person?
Our administrative office in Albuquerque welcomes scheduled visits from partners, donors, and press. To protect survivor privacy, clinical spaces are not open to visitors. Please email info@warsurvivors.org to arrange a time.
